SBA Offers Disaster Assistance for March 25-26, 2021 Storms

Businesses and residents in the declared area can now apply for low-interest disaster loans from the SBA. The disaster declaration covers Bibb, Calhoun, Clay, Hale, Jefferson, Perry, Randolph and Shelby counties in Alabama, which are eligible for both Physical and Economic Injury Disaster Loans from the SBA. Small businesses and most private nonprofit organizations in the following adjacent counties are eligible to apply only for SBA Economic Injury Disaster Loans: Blount, Chambers, Cherokee, Chilton, Cleburne, Coosa, Dallas, Etowah, Greene, Marengo, Saint Clair, Tallapoosa, Talladega, Tuscaloosa and Walker in Alabama.

The filing deadline to return applications for physical property damage is June 25, 2021. The deadline to return economic injury applications is Jan. 26, 2022.

For more information, see the related documentation and press release.
